Example 64: Epsilon Forces Event Ordering
===========================================
Processes: P1, P2
Events: 4
Epsilon: 1.0
Expected verdict: SATISFIED

With epsilon=1.0, t(e3)-t(e2) = 3.0-1.0 = 2.0 > 1.0,
so e2 must precede e3. Also t(e2)-t(e1) = 1.0 is NOT > 1.0,
so e1 and e2 remain concurrent.

Partial Order Graph:
--------------------

    P1          P2
    |           |
   e0(a)      e1(b)
    |       .   |
   e2(a)  .    |
    :   .      |
    : (eps)    |
    :..........> e3(b)

Cross-process edges:
  e2 -> e3  (Dt=2.0 > eps=1.0)

e0 and e1 are concurrent (same timestamp, no msg).
e2 and e3 are ordered by epsilon. The formula 'b' evaluates
at the maximal frontier which includes e3(b), so SATISFIED.
